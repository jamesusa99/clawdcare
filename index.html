Global digital health is heading toward $946B by 2030 (industry estimates). Users already pay for longitudinal intelligence—Function Health proved the category. The open question is who owns the trust layer: local-first, family-shaped, and agent-guarded.

Positioning

Health operating layer—not a lab coupon, not a generic chatbot.

What we are

  • A local-first container for fragmented records (PDFs, DICOM summaries, labs, wearables).
  • A longitudinal system: baseline → LifeClock layer → 90-day actions → retest comparison.
  • A family-shaped product: multi-member views and coordinated reminders.

What we are not

  • Not “just another bundled checkup marketplace.”
  • Not cloud-first archive of sensitive history without user intent.
  • Not an unconstrained medical agent with silent write access to your life.

Market gap

Fragmentation, weak retest loops, and privacy fatigue.

Traditional care excels after something breaks; it rarely turns years of scattered data into a calm, continuous system. DTC leaders proved willingness to pay for longitudinal insight—Function Health scaled labs + imaging + wearables + records and raised significant growth rounds.

Scale and convenience belong to the cloud; trust, family context, controllable agents, and willingness to import deeply belong to a credentialed local hub.

Platform

Four layers: ingest → intelligence → action → bounded ecosystem.

ClawdCare is a health operating layer: durable record + interpretive engine + execution loop + guarded extensions.

1 · Data plane

Auto-import physicals, lab PDFs, imaging summaries, wearables, household profiles. OCR, field mapping, structured longitudinal record.

2 · Health intelligence

LifeClock: biological age trend, cluster assignment (64 groups), driver decomposition, long-horizon risk hints, retest prioritization.

3 · Action loop

Next retest windows, missing data, behavior nudges, clinician talking points, family-aware reminders—insight → execution.

4 · Ecosystem (guarded)

OpenClaw-style skills with medical boundaries: allow list · read-default · dual confirm · sensitive-field isolation · full audit trail.

Economics

Five revenue layers + disciplined price architecture.

Hardware pulls households into a high-trust data container; subscription + partner conversion + audited skills build ARPU. A single $99/mo SKU would lose to established $149–499/yr anchors—so we split Hub vs Pro.

Layer 1$199 device — acquisition & trust anchor (margin is not the point early).
Layer 2Hub — $19/mo or $199/yr · imports, trends, baseline risk cues, annual retest planning.
